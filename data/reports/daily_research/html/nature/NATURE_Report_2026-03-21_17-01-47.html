Abstract

The thymus is essential for establishing T cell diversity early in life, but undergoes profound involution with age and has therefore traditionally been regarded as largely nonfunctional in adults<sup>1,2</sup>. Here we propose that preserving thymic functionality is integral to adult health and longevity. We developed a deep learning framework to quantify thymic health from routine radiographic images and evaluated its association with longevity and risk of major age-associated diseases in two large prospective cohorts of asymptomatic adults: the National Lung Screening Trial (n = 25,031) and the Framingham Heart Study (n = 2,581). In both cohorts, thymic health varied markedly across the population. In the National Lung Screening Trial, higher thymic health was consistently associated with lower all-cause mortality, reduced lung cancer incidence and lower cardiovascular mortality over 12 years of follow-up after adjustment for age, sex, smoking and comorbidities. In the independent Framingham Heart Study cohort, higher thymic health was significantly associated with reduced cardiovascular mortality, independent of age, sex and smoking. Thymic health was further linked to systemic inflammation and metabolic dysregulation, and associated with modifiable lifestyle factors including smoking, obesity and physical activity. Together, these findings reposition the thymus as a central regulator of immune-mediated ageing and disease susceptibility in adulthood, highlighting its potential as a target for preventive and regenerative strategies to promote healthy ageing and longevity.

摘要翻译

尽管免疫疗法已彻底改变了癌症治疗，许多患者仍获益有限，这凸显了对改进生物标志物¹的迫切需求。虽然免疫疗法的核心在于释放T细胞²，但现有生物标志物大多以肿瘤为中心，主要忽视了宿主免疫能力。胸腺是T细胞成熟的关键免疫器官，我们假设胸腺功能与免疫治疗效果相关³。本研究表明，胸腺健康度（一种胸腺功能的影像学评估指标）在多种癌症类型中与免疫治疗效果密切相关。通过将深度学习框架应用于常规计算机断层扫描图像，我们在一个包含3,476名接受免疫检查点抑制剂治疗患者的泛癌队列中量化了胸腺健康度。在非小细胞肺癌患者中，较高的胸腺健康度与疾病进展风险和全因死亡率降低相关。这些关联在程序性死亡配体1（PD-L1）和肿瘤突变负荷的不同临床相关水平上均保持显著。在前瞻性TRACERx肺癌研究中，胸腺健康度与T细胞受体多样性及T细胞受体切除环呈正相关，并与免疫系统信号通路相关联，这支持了影像学胸腺健康度可作为胸腺活性和适应性免疫能力的替代指标。对黑色素瘤、乳腺癌或肾癌患者的分析证明了其泛癌种相关性。综上，这些发现确立了胸腺健康度作为一个先前未被认识的、与肿瘤类型无关的免疫疗法疗效决定因素，对患者分层、治疗时机选择以及精准免疫肿瘤学中免疫再生策略的开发具有潜在意义。

Abstract

Although immunotherapy has revolutionized cancer treatment, many patients still experience limited benefit, highlighting the urgent need for improved biomarkers<sup>1</sup>. Although immunotherapy is founded on unleashing T cells<sup>2</sup>, most existing biomarkers remain tumour-centric and mainly overlook host immune competence. The thymus is a key immune organ that is crucial for T cell maturation, and we hypothesized that thymic functionality is associated with immunotherapy outcomes<sup>3</sup>. Here we show that thymic health, a radiographic measure of thymic functionality, is strongly associated with immunotherapy outcomes across several cancer types. Using a deep-learning framework applied to routine computed tomography images, we quantified thymic health in a pan-cancer cohort of 3,476 patients receiving immune checkpoint inhibitors. In patients with non-small cell lung cancer, higher thymic health was associated with reduced risks of progression and all-cause mortality. These associations remained significant across clinically relevant levels of programmed death ligand 1 (PD-L1) and tumour mutation burden. In the prospective TRACERx lung cancer study, thymic health was positively associated with T cell receptor diversity and T cell receptor excision circles, and correlated with immune-system signalling pathways, supporting radiographic thymic health as a proxy for thymic activity and adaptive immune competence. Analysis across patients with melanoma, breast cancer or renal cancer demonstrated pan-cancer relevance. Together, these findings identify thymic health as a previously unrecognized, tumour-agnostic determinant of immunotherapy efficacy, with potential implications for patient stratification, treatment timing and the development of immune-rejuvenating strategies in precision immuno-oncology.
